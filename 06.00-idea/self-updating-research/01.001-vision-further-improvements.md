# Vision: future improvements and deferred infrastructure

> Items deferred from the initial research automation vision. Each has a **trigger condition** — the signal that moves it from backlog to active work.

---

## 🗄️ Deferred research infrastructure

### Structured claim database

**What:** A persistent store of validated claims — `{claim_id, claim_text, sources[], confidence, domain, extraction_date, last_verified, used_in_articles[]}`. Enables reuse of previously validated claims across research cycles without re-triangulating.

**Why deferred:** No research automation exists yet to produce claims. Building the schema before the pipeline runs produces empty infrastructure. The initial implementation can use in-memory claim sets per cycle.

**Trigger condition:** When research cycles begin producing >100 validated claims per domain per month, persistent storage justifies its cost.

**Vision reference:** R-R1-source-triangulation, R-R5-knowledge-continuity

---

### Source content archive

**What:** Local archive of fetched source content with content hashes and fetch timestamps. Enables detection of source changes, broken links, and retroactive validation.

**Why deferred:** Initial research can re-fetch sources as needed. Archiving adds value when source stability becomes a problem or when retroactive audits are needed.

**Trigger condition:** When >10% of source links in published articles become broken within 6 months, or when a retroactive audit is needed.

**Vision reference:** Source availability risk in Risks and tensions

---

### Domain configuration files

**What:** Formal YAML/JSON configuration per domain (`research-config.yaml`) declaring sources, cadence, confidence thresholds, and scope boundaries.

**Why deferred:** The initial implementation can embed domain configuration in agent/prompt files (same pattern as PE system). Formal config files add value when the number of domains exceeds what's manageable inline.

**Trigger condition:** When >5 domains are actively configured, or when the research engine is packaged for portable delivery.

**Vision reference:** R-R6-domain-configuration

---

### Research outcome log

**What:** `research-outcome-log.jsonl` — structured log of research cycle outcomes: `{cycle_id, domain, claims_discovered, claims_validated, articles_drafted, articles_published, human_corrections, hallucinations_caught_by_layer}`

**Why deferred:** No automated cycles exist yet. The initial implementation logs outcomes in markdown (matching current PE practice).

**Trigger condition:** When automated research cycles begin, or when outcome volume exceeds what markdown logging handles (~20 entries per month).

**Vision reference:** Progressive learning (inherited from PE vision R-G3)

---

## 🧠 Advanced reasoning capabilities

### Cross-domain synthesis

**What:** Automatically detecting when findings in one domain have implications for another. Example: a prompt engineering finding about context rot could inform technical writing guidance about document length.

**Why deferred:** Requires validated research in multiple domains simultaneously. The single-domain pipeline must work reliably before cross-domain connections are attempted.

**Trigger condition:** When ≥3 domains have active research cycles producing validated claims, enabling cross-domain comparison.

**Vision reference:** Phase 3 Reason — "Connect" step

---

### Trend detection

**What:** Identifying directional shifts in a domain by tracking how topics, recommendations, and practices evolve over time. Example: detecting the shift from single-model to multi-model architectures in prompt engineering.

**Why deferred:** Requires longitudinal data — multiple research cycles producing claims over months. Single-cycle analysis can't detect trends.

**Trigger condition:** When a domain has ≥6 months of research cycle data with timestamped claims.

---

### Predictive insights

**What:** Extrapolating from trends to suggest what's likely to become important. Example: "Given that VS Code is adding more agent execution contexts each release, expect background agent capabilities to expand."

**Why deferred:** Predictions are inherently speculative. The system must demonstrate reliable established/supported claims before attempting predictions. Speculative content carries the highest hallucination risk.

**Trigger condition:** When trend detection is operational AND human reviewers explicitly request predictive analysis for a domain.

---

## 📦 Delivery vehicles

### Research MCP server

**What:** Package the research pipeline as an MCP server exposing tools: `discover_sources`, `validate_claims`, `reason_about_findings`, `synthesize_article`. Callable from chat, prompts, and agents.

**Why deferred:** The pipeline logic is designed first as agent/prompt workflows. MCP packaging requires reimplementing in a runtime (Node.js/Python) or wrapping prompt invocations as tools.

**Trigger condition:** When the research pipeline is validated in one domain and ready for portable deployment to other repositories.

---

### Scheduled research agent

**What:** An agent that runs on a schedule (daily/weekly per domain) and produces research reports without manual invocation.

**Why deferred:** Requires event-driven execution infrastructure (VS Code tasks, GitHub Actions, or MCP scheduling). Manual invocation works for initial validation.

**Trigger condition:** When the research pipeline produces consistently reliable results and manual invocation becomes a bottleneck.

---

### Research dashboard

**What:** A visual summary of research status across domains — what's been discovered, what's pending validation, what's been published, knowledge gap coverage.

**Why deferred:** Dashboard development is high effort relative to the initial goal of producing reliable articles. A markdown summary file achieves the same goal at lower cost.

**Trigger condition:** When >5 domains are actively monitored and the markdown summary becomes unwieldy.

---

## 📊 Quality feedback loops

### Reader analytics integration

**What:** Track which research-produced articles get the most views, which sections readers spend time on, and which articles have high bounce rates. Feed this back into research prioritization.

**Why deferred:** Requires analytics infrastructure (likely Quarto site analytics or similar). The initial system prioritizes based on domain configuration and knowledge gaps, not reader behavior.

**Trigger condition:** When the Quarto site has analytics enabled and >6 months of data.

---

### Correction tracking

**What:** Structured tracking of corrections made to research-produced articles — which hallucination layer should have caught the error, which domain, which source type. Feeds back into pipeline tuning.

**Why deferred:** Requires published research-produced articles AND a correction workflow. The initial system treats human review as the correction mechanism.

**Trigger condition:** When >20 research-produced articles have been published and correction patterns are detectable.

## 🌐 Domain expansion

### Priority domains for initial research

| Domain | Path | Velocity | Initial priority |
|--------|------|----------|-----------------|
| Prompt engineering | `03.00-tech/05.02-prompt-engineering` | High (weekly) | ✅ First — most coverage, fastest evolution |
| Technical writing | `03.00-tech/40.00-technical-writing` | Medium (monthly) | ✅ Second — established article-writing system |
| Programming languages | `03.00-tech/04.01-programming-languages` | Medium (monthly) | Third — .NET/C# version releases |
| Azure | `03.00-tech/02.01-azure` | High (weekly) | Fourth — broad scope, many services |
| Authentication | `03.00-tech/01.01-authentication` | Low (quarterly) | Fifth — slow-moving, standards-based |

**Why phased:** Each domain requires source list curation, scope definition, and initial knowledge mapping. Activating all at once spreads effort thin and makes failure analysis harder.

**Trigger for expansion:** When the first domain (prompt engineering) has completed ≥3 successful research cycles with human-reviewed articles meeting quality standards.
