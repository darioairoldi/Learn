# Vision Update Plan: Deduplicate and Tighten the Vision Document

> Reduce ~710 lines to ~500 by eliminating redundancy — zero concepts removed, one canonical location per concept, cross-references everywhere else.

---

## The problem

The vision grew through 22 incremental changes. Each change added content where it was mechanically needed — a rationale row, a design principle, an Assess layer paragraph. The result: many concepts are fully described in 2-6 places, with ~50% overlap between the Rationale table, Design Principles, and Operational Layers.

**13 redundancy patterns** identified. Approximately **150-200 lines** can be removed by establishing one canonical location per concept and cross-referencing elsewhere.

---

## The deduplication principle

Each concept follows the **WHY → WHAT → HOW** pipeline:

| Layer | Contains | Length guide |
|---|---|---|
| **Rationale table** (WHY) | 2-3 line motivation + design implication pointer | Short — rationale only |
| **Design principle** (WHAT) | Full canonical definition with rules | Medium — complete rule |
| **Operational layer** (HOW) | Applied mechanics specific to that layer, cross-referencing the principle | Only adds context-specific detail |

When the rationale restates the principle, or the operational layer restates both — cut the restatement, add a cross-reference.

**What**: R-P4, R-P5, R-S1, R-S7, R-S8, R-S9, R-S10 each contain 3-15 lines of content that's fully restated in their corresponding Design Principle. Compress each to: 2-3 line rationale (WHY this matters) + "See [principle name] for the full rule."

- R-P4 → "Rules, rationales, and examples are separable" principle

## Safety check: What stays complete

After deduplication, every concept still has:
1. A **canonical location** with the full definition/rule
2. A **rationale** explaining WHY (2-3 lines in the rationale table)
3. **Operational application** in the relevant layer (Assess/Execute/Detect)
4. **Cross-references** connecting the three

What's removed: restating the same concept's definition/argument in multiple locations. The pipeline becomes WHY → WHAT → HOW with pointers instead of WHY (full) → WHAT (full restatement) → HOW (full restatement again).
