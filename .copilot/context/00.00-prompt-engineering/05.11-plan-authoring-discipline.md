# Plan authoring discipline

**Purpose**: Single source of truth for the discipline that makes a plan file safe to execute — how an author drives a plan to *readiness*, how the plan moves through its *lifecycle*, and the *actionability gate* that the body must pass. The companion instruction [plan-execution.instructions.md](../../../.github/instructions/plan-execution.instructions.md) carries the terse normative MUSTs and points here for the reasoning and detail.

## Why writing comes last

The central rule: **authoring the actionable body is the LAST act, not the first.** A plan whose steps encode unresolved guesses produces misaligned or ambiguous work no matter how carefully the steps are later marked. So the author first drives every *blocking unknown* to resolution, and only then writes steps. A blocking unknown is any ambiguity, missing fact, or undecided option that would force a step to be written as a guess.

While any blocking unknown remains, the plan stays `status: draft` and holds only its goal, its § Open decisions, and its § Discovery list — no actionable body.

## The resolution ladder

Every blocking unknown MUST be driven through this ladder, in order. Each rung has a distinct tool posture:

1. **Resolve from evidence** *(read-only investigation)* — read code, files, and prior plans until a fact settles the unknown. This is the default first move; most unknowns are answerable from the workspace.
2. **Ask the user** *(interactive question)* — for any decision, preference, intent, or trade-off that evidence cannot settle, ASK before writing. Batch related questions into one exchange. NEVER guess a user intent to keep momentum.
3. **Bound as discovery** *(deferred, with a negative branch)* — ONLY for facts that genuinely cannot be decided until execution time, write a § Discovery item carrying a **defined negative branch** (`if absent → do X`). The negative branch is what makes a deferred probe safe: the plan proceeds deterministically whichever way the probe resolves.

An unknown that none of (1)–(3) resolves is a **STOP** condition. The body MUST NOT be written. The plan stays `draft` until the unknown is resolved or the user reshapes the goal.

> Investigative phrasing (verify / confirm / check / assess) is a smell in an actionable step. It is acceptable **only** when it carries a defined negative branch or lives in § Discovery gating its dependents — otherwise it is an unresolved unknown wearing the costume of an action.

## Lifecycle states and transitions

Plans move through four states; every transition is gated.

| Status | Meaning | Allowed transitions |
|---|---|---|
| `draft` | Readiness loop in progress — holds goal + § Open decisions + § Discovery only; **no actionable body yet** | → `actionable` (after the actionability gate passes) |
| `actionable` | Gate passed; actionable body written; ready to execute but not started | → `in-progress` (first executed step) or → `draft` (a gate condition regresses) |
| `in-progress` | At least one item executed and marked done | → `done` (all active items complete and exit criteria met) |
| `done` | All active items complete; exit criteria satisfied | terminal — further change needs a new plan |

Hard rules:

- A `draft` plan MUST NOT have any item executed.
- The actionable body MUST NOT be written while `status: draft` — it is authored **only** at the `draft`→`actionable` transition, once the gate passes.
- A `done` plan MUST NOT have items added or removed — surface follow-ups via a sibling plan.
- The frontmatter `status:` field MUST be updated *before* the corresponding work begins.

## The actionability gate (eight checks)

The gate runs **before** the body is written and again at the `draft`→`actionable` transition. Every check must pass:

1. **Goal alignment** — the `goal:` restates and matches the user's actual request; any narrowing is explicit, never silent. *(A plan that quietly shrinks the ask solves the wrong problem.)*
2. **Goal reachability** — the steps, executed in order, reach the goal with no undocumented work and no gap. *(Reachability is about the whole path, not individual steps.)*
3. **Execution determinism** — each step has exactly one reasonable execution; no decision is left for the executor to improvise. *(Determinism is what lets a different actor run the plan identically.)*
4. **Clarity & actionability** — investigative verbs (verify / confirm / check / assess) are NOT actionable unless they carry a defined negative branch or live in § Discovery gating their dependents.
5. **Unknown resolution** — every blocking unknown is resolved-by-evidence, asked-of-user, or bounded-as-discovery; none is guessed.
6. **Scope discipline** — no item exceeds the verbatim trigger; expansions live in § Park lot, not the active list.
7. **Coverage promise** — every in-scope goal item names a downstream landing (a path, a sibling-plan id, or `vision-only`), so nothing in scope silently disappears.
8. **Principle impact** *(vision-amendment plans only)* — every item is tagged per [vision-amendment.instructions.md](../../../.github/instructions/vision-amendment.instructions.md).

On any failure: **resolve first** (evidence → ask user), keep `status: draft`, then re-run the gate. NEVER write a body or execute a plan that has not passed.

## Open decisions, discovery, and park lot — three distinct buckets

These three lists look similar but answer different questions; keeping them distinct is what prevents scope creep and stalled plans:

| Bucket | Holds | In/out of scope | Keeps plan in `draft`? |
|---|---|---|---|
| § Open decisions | In-scope decisions awaiting evidence or a user answer | **In** scope | Yes — closing all is a gate precondition |
| § Discovery | Facts undecidable until execution, each with a defined negative branch | **In** scope | No — the negative branch makes it safe |
| § Park lot | Edge cases surfaced during authoring/execution that are out of scope | **Out** of scope | No |

Rules:

- Each § Open decision MUST name what resolves it and which steps it gates.
- Each § Discovery item MUST carry a defined negative branch.
- Each § Park lot item MUST carry a one-line disposition: `→ <sibling-plan-id>.md`, `→ defer`, or `→ closed: <reason>`.
- Migrating a § Park lot item into the active list drops the plan back to `draft` and re-runs the gate.

## Trigger points

The instruction is auto-loaded for any file matching `*plan*`. The gate fires at three moments:

- **On create** *(primary enforcement)* — before the body is authored, the resolution ladder + gate run as a blocking precondition. Writing is blocked until they pass.
- **On save** — when a plan file is modified and saved, the gate evaluates the current state and reports failures inline.
- **On ask** — when a user asks the agent to execute a plan, the gate runs as the first action, regardless of declared status.

The gate does NOT auto-promote `draft`→`actionable`. Promotion is an explicit author action after the gate passes.
